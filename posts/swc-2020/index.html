Software Carpentry June 2020

June 2, 2020 1-minute read

This week, I’m finishing up my first experience with a couple of Software Carpentry courses. This set of courses - one on Python and one on R - was organized for the Summer Student Fellows and incoming Joint Program students at WHOI.

This time around, I was a helper, but the experience was eye-opening and rewarding. In particular, I had the chance to teach one of the episodes myself during the Python course. It was pretty surprising how much harder it was to teach once the pressure was all on me, especially with the Zoom format that was necessary for this unusual summer. Suddenly, I wasn’t sure on pacing, or whether what I was saying was making sense, and I got tripped up a couple of times on mentioning something that hadn’t been taught yet. However, it was excellent practice, and I feel much more confident about learning to be a Software Carpentry instructor now.

I am fortunate to have been allotted a slot in Software Carpentry training starting later this month - excited to keep going with the organization after this initial look!
